# Topic Page Specification v1.0

## Status

Approved

---

# Goal

A Topic Page provides users with a complete overview of one of Spain's major concerns.

Its purpose is not to answer a single claim, but to answer questions such as:

* Is this really one of Spain's biggest problems?
* How serious is the situation?
* Why do people disagree about it?
* Which claims should I investigate?
* What do the available data show?

A successful Topic Page should leave users with a much better understanding of the overall issue before diving into individual claims.

---

# Design Principles

* Overview before detail.
* Visual-first.
* Easy to scan.
* Mobile-first.
* Data before opinion.
* Show complexity without overwhelming the user.
* Encourage exploration of individual claims.

---

# Estimated Reading Time

Main overview:

5–10 minutes.

The existing investigation remains available for users wanting additional depth.

---

# Page Structure

## 1. Hero

Purpose:

Immediately explain the state of the topic.

Contents:

* Topic title.
* Main question.
* Short answer.
* Estimated reading time.

Example:

# Inmigración

## ¿Está realmente España en una crisis migratoria?

La inmigración aporta beneficios importantes al país, pero también genera retos reales cuya intensidad depende del territorio, las políticas públicas y la capacidad de integración.

---

## 2. Current Situation

Purpose:

Summarise the current state of the topic in a few seconds.

Display:

* Overall assessment.
* Three key findings.
* Main supporting visual.

Example:

Situación actual

🟡 Situación compleja

Principales conclusiones

* España necesita inmigración para sostener parte de su mercado laboral.
* Algunas zonas experimentan presión sobre vivienda y servicios públicos.
* Muchos debates mezclan datos reales con interpretaciones simplificadas.

This section should provide immediate orientation before users begin exploring.

---

## 3. Why People Think This Is a Problem

Purpose:

Introduce the debate before presenting evidence.

Explain why many Spaniards perceive this issue as important.

Example:

¿Por qué muchas personas creen que este problema está empeorando?

Possible factors:

* Personal experiences.
* Media coverage.
* Social networks.
* Economic changes.
* Political discourse.
* Recent events.

This section explains the context behind public concern rather than judging it.

---

## 4. Popular Claims

Purpose:

Present the main debates related to this topic.

Display:

4–8 Claim Cards.

Examples for Immigration:

* ¿La inmigración aumenta la delincuencia?
* ¿Los inmigrantes reciben más ayudas?
* ¿España necesita inmigración?
* ¿La inmigración aumenta el precio de la vivienda?

Each card links directly to its Claim Page.

This section becomes the main navigation hub for the topic.

---

## 5. Key Statistics

Purpose:

Allow users to understand the topic visually.

The page should prioritise charts and visualisations over long text.

Possible visualisations:

* Line charts.
* Maps.
* Timelines.
* Comparisons.
* Rankings.
* Infographics.

Charts should appear embedded throughout the page rather than grouped together.

Each visual should answer one specific question.

---

## 6. Timeline

Purpose:

Provide historical context.

Display the major events that changed this topic over time.

Example:

2013

...

2020

...

2022

...

2025

...

The timeline should explain why today's situation differs from previous years.

---

## 7. Deep Investigation

Purpose:

Preserve the existing long-form research.

Implementation:

The current investigation should remain largely unchanged.

It should appear after the overview sections.

Users interested in greater detail can continue reading without leaving the page.

The long investigation should not be rewritten unless required for accuracy or clarity.

---

## 8. Rich Media (Future)

Not part of MVP.

The page should be designed to support curated media in future versions.

Possible content:

* Videos.
* Podcasts.
* Interviews.
* Documentary excerpts.
* Expert talks.

Rich media should be manually curated.

Automatically embedding external media is out of scope.

---

## 9. Lived Experiences (Future)

Not part of MVP.

Future versions may include curated real-world perspectives illustrating how different people experience the topic.

Examples:

* Teacher.
* Doctor.
* Police officer.
* Employer.
* Young renter.
* Immigrant worker.
* Local resident.

Stories should illustrate mechanisms rather than replace evidence.

Each story should explain:

* What this experience illustrates.
* What it does not prove.

---

## 10. Navigation

After finishing the page, users should be encouraged to continue exploring.

Display:

### Related Claims

Claims belonging to the same topic.

### Other Topics

Navigation to the remaining major concerns.

Both navigation sections should be visible.

---

# Visual Identity

Every topic should have:

* Unique colour.
* Unique icon.
* Unique illustration.

The visual identity should remain consistent across:

* Homepage.
* Topic Page.
* Claim Pages.
* Shareable assets.

---

# Removed from MVP

The following features are intentionally excluded:

* AI assistant.
* Comments.
* User discussions.
* Personalisation.
* Live statistics.
* Automatic trend detection.

# Success Criteria

A successful Topic Page should allow users to:

* Quickly understand the current state of the topic.
* Understand why the issue generates public concern.
* Discover the most important claims.
* Learn primarily through visuals.
* Continue into deeper investigations if interested.
* Leave with a much better overall understanding of the problem.

---

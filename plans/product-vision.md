# Product Vision v1.0

## Status

Approved

---

# Vision

El País Está Fatal helps people build a more accurate understanding of Spain.

Instead of telling users what to think, the project helps them understand what we know, what we do not know, why complex issues generate disagreement, and what evidence supports different explanations.

The website exists to transform public debate from simple narratives into informed understanding.

---

# Why We Exist

Public debate about Spain is increasingly shaped by headlines, social media, political messaging and isolated statistics.

Many important discussions become reduced to simple slogans such as:

* "Spain is collapsing."
* "Immigration is the problem."
* "Taxes are too high."
* "Healthcare is broken."

Reality is usually more complex.

Evidence often exists, but it is fragmented, difficult to interpret or presented without enough context.

El País Está Fatal exists to organise that evidence into explanations that ordinary people can understand and share.

---

# The Problem We Solve

People rarely lack information.

They lack context.

A single statistic, headline or personal experience is often used to explain an entire national issue.

Our goal is not to collect more information.

Our goal is to connect information into a clearer understanding.

---

# Target Audience

The project is designed for people who have encountered a claim about Spain and genuinely want to understand whether it reflects reality.

Typical situations include:

* Reading a headline.
* Seeing a viral social media post.
* Hearing a political statement.
* Having a discussion with friends or family.
* Wondering whether a commonly repeated claim is actually supported by evidence.

The website should meet users at that moment of curiosity.

---

# Mission

Help people understand Spain through evidence, context and visual explanations.

The website should make complex public issues easier to explore without oversimplifying them.

---

# What Makes EPETF Different

El País Está Fatal is not built around articles.

It is built around questions.

Users usually begin with a specific claim.

The website then provides:

* Evidence.
* Context.
* Competing explanations.
* Related questions.
* Broader topic overviews.

The objective is not simply to answer one claim, but to help users gradually build a more complete understanding of Spain.

---

# Core Principles

## Evidence before opinion

Arguments should always be supported by reliable evidence whenever possible.

---

## Context before conclusions

Facts presented without context often lead to misleading interpretations.

The website should explain what the evidence means, not simply display it.

---

## Curiosity before certainty

The goal is to encourage exploration rather than reinforce certainty.

Users should leave understanding why many public debates are more complex than they initially appear.

---

## Visual before textual

Whenever possible, ideas should be explained visually before long written explanations.

Charts, comparisons, timelines and illustrations should become the primary language of the website.

---

## Simplicity before completeness

The first explanation should be easy to understand.

Additional depth should always be available for users who want it.

---

## Sharing knowledge

The project should make accurate explanations as easy to share as simplified narratives.

Users should be able to reuse evidence-based arguments in everyday conversations.

---

# What EPETF Is Not

The project is not:

* A traditional newspaper.
* A political campaign.
* A debate forum.
* A social network.
* A government transparency portal.
* A generic encyclopedia.
* A conventional fact-checking website.

It may borrow ideas from these products, but it has a different purpose.

---

# Product Philosophy

The website should answer two levels of questions.

Level 1:

Individual claims.

Example:

"¿La inmigración aumenta la delincuencia?"

Level 2:

Broader understanding.

Example:

"¿Cómo está realmente la inmigración en España?"

Claim Pages answer individual questions.

Topic Pages answer broader questions.

Together they help users build a richer understanding of Spain.

---

# Success

The project succeeds when users leave with a better understanding than when they arrived.

Success is not measured only by:

* Page views.
* Reading time.
* Social shares.

The most important outcome is whether users better understand:

* What the evidence says.
* Why people disagree.
* Which parts of a debate are facts.
* Which parts remain uncertain.
* How different issues connect to one another.

---

# Long-Term Vision

El País Está Fatal aims to become the place people consult before forming an opinion about one of Spain's major public issues.

The website should become known for:

* Clear explanations.
* Reliable evidence.
* Honest uncertainty.
* High-quality visual communication.
* Respect for complexity.

As the project grows, new topics, media formats and technologies may be added.

However, the core mission should remain unchanged:

**Help people build a more accurate understanding of Spain through evidence, context and visual explanations.**

---

# Guiding Question

Every future feature should answer one question before being implemented:

> **Will this help users better understand Spain?**

If the answer is no, the feature does not belong in the product.

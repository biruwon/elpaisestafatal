## Overall verdict

You have built something **substantively strong but behaviorally weak**.

It is already a credible research archive: the site distinguishes perception from objective measurement, explains limitations, uses mostly primary sources and provides unusually nuanced analyses of politically charged subjects. 

But it is not yet clear why an ordinary person would:

1. arrive today,
2. obtain a useful answer quickly,
3. share it,
4. and return next week.

At present, the primary experience is: **choose one of 14 topics and read a very long dossier**. Politics contains 25 chapters, employment 24, immigration 20 and extremism 30. That is valuable for highly motivated readers, journalists and researchers, but too demanding for most people arriving from Google, WhatsApp, TikTok or X. 

My central recommendation is:

> **Stop presenting the project primarily as 14 dossiers. Present it as the fastest trustworthy place to check a claim about Spain.**

The dossiers should remain underneath as the evidence base.

---

# 1. Change the main user job

The current homepage asks users to “explore concerns.” 

That is not a strong recurring need. People rarely wake up thinking:

> “Today I want to explore a dossier about Spain.”

They do think:

- “Is it true that immigrants receive more benefits?”
- “Why can’t I afford a flat?”
- “Has crime actually increased?”
- “Are Spaniards paying more taxes than other Europeans?”
- “Is employment really at record levels?”
- “Which party has more corruption cases?”
- “Someone sent me this chart. Is it misleading?”

Make **those questions** the primary product.

### New homepage hero

Instead of starting with only:

> España no cabe en un titular.

Use something closer to:

> **¿España está realmente fatal?**  
> Busca una afirmación, una cifra o un problema. Te mostramos qué sabemos, qué no sabemos y de dónde salen los datos.

Then provide a large input:

> **Escribe o pega una afirmación**  
> “Los inmigrantes viven de ayudas…”

Below it, show clickable examples:

- ¿Hay más delincuencia que hace diez años?
- ¿Por qué sube tanto la vivienda?
- ¿España cobra demasiados impuestos?
- ¿El empleo creado es realmente estable?

You do not initially need an AI system capable of answering everything. Start with curated search and manually produced answers.

---

# 2. Create a “60-second answer” above every dossier

The pages currently begin with a statistic and methodological warning, followed almost immediately by a large research investigation. The opening is rigorous, but the user still has to assemble the conclusion themselves. 

Every topic should begin with a compact decision layer.

## Example: Housing

### What is happening?

**Housing access has deteriorated quickly, especially in growing cities and coastal areas.**

- Prices: worsening rapidly
- Rent affordability: worsening
- New supply: insufficient in high-demand areas
- Social housing: far below comparable European levels
- Empty homes: real, but often located elsewhere
- Main uncertainty: how much each individual cause contributes locally

Then:

> **The conclusion in one sentence:** Spain does not mainly lack buildings everywhere; it lacks permanently available, affordable homes where employment and population are growing.

That conclusion already exists inside your research, but it is buried several screens down. 

Give users three choices:

- **Understand it in 60 seconds**
- **See the evidence**
- **Read the complete investigation**

This layered structure would serve casual readers without sacrificing depth.

# 3. Turn each controversial claim into a standalone page

This would likely become your main acquisition mechanism.

For example:

`/verificaciones/inmigrantes-ayudas`

## Recommended format

### Claim

“Immigrants come to Spain to live on benefits.”

### Assessment

**Misleading generalisation**

### What is true

Some low-income immigrant households receive means-tested support, as do qualifying Spanish households.

### What is missing

Access depends on residence, income, family situation, registration and, for contributory benefits, previous contributions.

### Relevant scale

More than three million foreign workers were affiliated with Social Security at the end of 2025.

### What the data cannot prove

An aggregate employment number does not establish the fiscal balance of every immigrant group.

### Sources

Direct links, dates, table references and downloadable data.

### Shareable response

> La afirmación mezcla ayudas condicionadas por renta con nacionalidad. Hay más de tres millones de afiliados extranjeros, pero el balance varía según edad, salario, familia y tiempo de residencia.

Your immigration dossier already contains the substance required for several dozen of these pages. 

These pages would:

- rank for specific Google questions;
- be easier to share in WhatsApp conversations;
- let users answer misinformation without linking to a 20-chapter article;
- give social-media posts a precise destination;
- gradually create a reusable public knowledge base.

# 4. Fix the comparability of the homepage ranking

This is the most important methodological UX issue.

The homepage visually presents a ranking led by politics at 37.5%, employment at 31.7% and housing at 25.1%. It explains that percentages normally represent the “first problem,” except when categories have been grouped. 

However:

- Politics is a weighted union of five categories mentioned in any of three answers.
- Employment is a union of two categories mentioned in any position.
- Extremism is also a union of two categories.
- Housing is shown as the first problem cited.

These figures are individually explained correctly, but they are not directly comparable as a single league table. 

A careful reader may understand the caveat. Most visitors will simply see “politics 37.5 versus housing 25.1.”

## Better options

### Option A: Use one consistent measure

Rank all concerns by:

- first answer only; or
- mentioned in any of the three answers.

This is the cleanest solution.

### Option B: Provide a toggle

**How should concerns be measured?**

- First concern mentioned
- Mentioned at any point
- Grouped subject areas

### Option C: Stop calling it a ranking

Present:

> Concerns cannot all be compared directly because some combine overlapping categories.

Group them into broad concern families without ordinal numbering.

I would choose the toggle. It makes the methodology understandable and provides an interactive reason to explore.

# 5. Clearly separate facts, calculations, interpretations and proposals

The project promises “data, not trenches,” but some dossiers move seamlessly from measured statistics into strong political interpretation.

The politics page, for example, includes direct assessments of Sánchez, Feijóo, Vox, Sumar, the media and the functioning of institutions. Much of it is thoughtful and balanced, but these sections visually sit at the same level as sourced statistics. 

That creates unnecessary vulnerability. A critic can say:

> “This is an opinion site disguised as data.”

Not because the analysis is necessarily wrong, but because the epistemic category is unclear.

Use explicit labels:

- **Measured fact**
- **Calculation by El país está fatal**
- **Interpretation**
- **Disputed interpretation**
- **Policy proposal**
- **Insufficient evidence**

For causal statements, show a confidence level:

| Possible cause | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Lack of housing construction in growing areas | Strong |
| Tourist accommodation | Strong locally, limited nationally |
| Foreign buyers | Important in particular coastal markets |
| Investment funds | Visible, but not the majority of ownership |
| Immigration | Adds demand; does not explain supply failure |

This would make the site more intellectually honest and more resistant to partisan attacks.

# 6. Make trust a visible product feature

Your methodology says that you prioritise primary sources, state limitations and intend to record corrections publicly. 

That is good, but users should not have to visit the methodology page to discover it.

Every page should prominently display:

- Last reviewed date
- Author
- Data reviewer
- Sources checked
- Original dataset
- Calculation method
- Download calculation
- Revision history
- Report an error
- Funding and conflicts of interest

At the moment, the site identifies itself only as an independent project, and the contact page says the domain mailbox has not yet been configured. That can make a serious project feel unfinished or anonymous. 

Publish a real **About this project** page answering:

- Who is Antonio?
- Why did you create it?
- Do you belong to or work for a political party?
- How is it funded?
- Can sponsors influence conclusions?
- How are disputed claims reviewed?
- What happens when a source changes?
- Is the code or research repository public?

For sensitive political material, named responsibility will produce more trust than institutional-looking anonymity.

## There also appears to be a source-display problem

The politics dossier cites the judiciary and the European Commission in its body, but the concluding “De dónde sale esta ficha” section lists only three sources. The immigration dossier uses Interior, the OECD and Eurostat throughout, but its concluding source list displays only the CIS and INE. 

Even when every inline link works, that ending implies the source inventory is incomplete. Fix this before promoting the site heavily.

# 7. Show what changed, not only what exists

A static dossier can be visited once. A changing dashboard creates a habit.

Add a section to the homepage:

## What changed this month?

- Housing prices: new quarterly figure
- Unemployment: up/down from previous quarter
- Immigration: latest population estimate
- Crime: latest quarterly balance
- Healthcare: waiting-list update
- Public concern: biggest change in the latest CIS survey

Each update should answer:

1. What changed?
2. Is it meaningful or normal variation?
3. Does it alter the previous conclusion?
4. Which pages were updated?

Then allow:

> Follow housing  
> Follow employment  
> Receive the monthly Spain update

A weekly email might be too demanding initially. A concise monthly email could be enough:

> **Five things that actually changed in Spain this month.**

That is a clear reason to subscribe.

# 8. Resolve the extreme difference in topic depth

Some dossiers are enormous: politics has 25 chapters, employment 24 and extremism 30. By contrast, taxes and healthcare currently contain one principal statistical section followed by their sources. 

This produces an inconsistent promise. A user cannot know whether clicking a concern will open a comprehensive investigation or a preliminary data card.

Label their status:

- **Full investigation**
- **Data briefing**
- **In progress**
- **Last updated**
- **Next planned expansion**

Do not pretend that all 14 are equivalent.

I would actively feature only the three to five strongest investigations until the others reach a minimum standard. Depth inconsistency is more damaging than having fewer topics.

# 9. Add geography and personal relevance

National averages are often too abstract to be useful.

Let people choose:

- Autonomous community
- Province
- Age group
- Homeowner/renter
- Employed/unemployed
- Eventually, income band

Then transform:

> “853,509 people are waiting for surgery”

into:

> “In Andalucía, the average wait for this category is X, compared with Spain and the best-performing communities.”

Or:

> “Housing prices rose nationally, but here is what happened in Málaga, Jaén and Madrid.”

Start with autonomous-community filtering, not municipality-level data. It will make housing, unemployment, poverty, healthcare and crime materially more useful.

A personalised landing page could eventually say:

> **Your Spain:** Andalucía · 30–39 · renter  
> These are the indicators most relevant to you.

Do not require an account. Save selections locally, as your privacy page already describes for topic selections.

# 10. Turn sharing into a first-class feature

A generic “share page” button will not be enough. People share **arguments**, not repositories.

Every key finding should produce:

### A square visual

For Instagram and WhatsApp:

> **36,775 irregular arrivals in 2025**  
> More than 10 million Spanish residents were born abroad.  
> These are different magnitudes and should not be confused.

### A short text response

For X, Bluesky or comments.

### A chart

With title, unit, dates, caveat and source embedded in the image.

### A citation card

For journalists:

> Copy data · Copy citation · Download CSV · Embed chart

Your strongest distribution channel may be people using your material inside existing conversations, not people independently visiting the homepage.

---

# 11. Use the name more intelligently

**El país está fatal** is memorable, emotionally recognisable and highly shareable.

But it can also communicate:

- catastrophism;
- anti-government sentiment;
- a predetermined conclusion;
- another grievance-oriented political site.

I would not necessarily rename it. Instead, make the tension explicit:

> **El país está fatal. ¿Seguro?**

or:

> **El país está fatal — compruébalo.**

The brand becomes a question placed under examination, rather than your editorial conclusion.

That fits your existing “datos, no trincheras” positioning better.

# Recommended dossier structure

For housing, immigration, employment and politics:

1. **Answer in one sentence**
2. **Status:** improving, worsening, mixed or uncertain
3. **Three to five essential facts**
4. **What is true / misleading / unknown**
5. **Trend over time**
6. **Differences by territory and population**
7. **Main explanations, ranked by evidence**
8. **Common claims checked**
9. **Policy options with benefits, costs and constraints**
10. **What evidence could change the conclusion**
11. **Complete investigation**
12. **Sources, calculations and revisions**

Your current chapter material mostly fits sections 6–11. The missing layer is sections 1–5.

# What I would not build yet

Do not begin with:

- user accounts;
- open comments;
- a political social network;
- an unrestricted AI chatbot;
- all municipalities;
- daily news coverage;
- automatic scoring of every politician;
- dozens of new dossiers.

An unrestricted chatbot would be especially risky. One weak or invented answer could damage the credibility earned by the careful dossiers.

Start with curated questions and retrieval only from approved pages and datasets.

# Measure usefulness, not traffic

Your main metric should not be raw page views.

Track a **useful-session rate**: the percentage of visits in which someone does at least one meaningful action:

- reads a concise answer;
- expands the evidence;
- visits an original source;
- copies a response;
- downloads or shares a chart;
- subscribes to an update;
- returns to an updated topic.

Also measure:

- search questions with no good answer;
- percentage reaching the full dossier;
- source-link clicks;
- shares per claim page;
- return rate after an update;
- “helped me understand” responses;
- corrections submitted and resolved.

## The single most important shift

Your present product says:

> **Here is a large, rigorous investigation. Read it.**

The stronger product would say:

> **Tell me what you just heard about Spain. I’ll help you understand whether it is true, misleading, uncertain or missing context—and you can inspect every source.**

That product has a clear entry point, a repeatable use case and a natural distribution mechanism. Your existing investigations are a very strong foundation for it; the next work should be packaging, trust architecture and recurring utility rather than producing even more long-form content.
